Daily Interactions

Comparing the everyday material culture of an Indian raised in India with an Indian raised America

Morning ____ 10:00am ____ 11:00am ____ 2:00pm ____ 3:00pm ____ 4:00pm ____ 5:00pm ____ 8:00pm ____

7:00 am

Slow Breakfast

"My mother always keeps a dabba with an assortment of spices. This is an essential item in every Indian household. It's difficult to find these spices in the US so my food lacks flavor"

8:00 am

Chai vs. Coffee

“I look forward to drinking my sweet, creamy chai every morning with my family. I don’t have time for this process in America. A make a quick cup of coffee with my Nespresso”

"In India the process of making tea is slow and systematic. It beings with boiling water, adding the leaves, straining the mix and cooling it using a tossing motion. In America, I just pop a pod into the machine and my coffee is ready"

11:00 am

Scroll Break

"The first customary scroll break of the day. Instagram was an American obsession that eventually trickled down to India. Now,the incessant need to constantly check instagram is independent of the country or culture I live in."

11:00 am

Shared Insecurities

"Social media has the same effect on people across the word. Constantly comparing myself and idolizing a better would makes me feel worse about myself, no matter where I am"

4:00 pm

Laundry

“I’m embarrassed to say I didn’t know how to use the washing machine and dryer before going to America. In India, someone always does these things for you”

5:00 pm

Cleaning

“The same goes for cleaning, we cleaned our house in India with one broom. In America,
there is a cleaning product for every surface, every stain and spill”

7:00 am

Reminder Reminder!!

“I eagerly wait the clock to to turn 4 to go out and play with my friends in India. In America, I set alarms to remind me to go out for a run"

4:00 pm

Outdoor time

“In India, as soon as I got back from school, I put on my shoes and played badminton with my friends. Everyone got along and bonded over the game. It’s hard to find a community here"
